The receipt at the all-night pharmacy jammed twice, then unspooled across the counter in one long ribbon. She kept reading it, unhurried, while the cashier rang up the next customer, and something in her face wouldn't quite settle — not a smile exactly, more like one trying out different positions. He caught her at it a second time and looked away first, back down at the register, as if the fluorescent light might be responsible. He didn't ask what was funny. It was two in the morning, and everyone under that light looked like they might be seeing things, including him.
