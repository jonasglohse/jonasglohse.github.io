The trail split without a sign, and the third picked left with a confidence nobody bothered to question. He stopped at every switchback to count them out loud, and never landed on the same number twice. She reached the ridge first and was already taking a photo by the time the other two caught up, out of breath and a half-minute apart. She showed them the photo instead of turning around to the actual view, and for a second neither said which one they'd rather look at.
