He tasted the soup for the fourth time, adjusting nothing, as though tasting were itself the seasoning. Asked what was in it, he said, gravely, "developments," and kept stirring. The recipe wanted thyme; he had rosemary, and treated the swap like a decision made in a meeting rather than a shortage in the cupboard. By the end he had used every spoon in the kitchen, each one drying in its own puddle, a jury that never reached a verdict. He served it in the good bowls anyway, as though confidence were a garnish.
