Two men sat on the opposite bank, close enough that their shoulders nearly touched, saying nothing. The river moved the way it always does, indifferent and continuous, and a dragonfly landed on a branch above the water and was gone before you could be sure of it. One of the men laughed at something, though there had been no sound. The other looked at the water. It was the kind of afternoon that feels like it has been retrieved from somewhere, like something you were certain you had lost.
