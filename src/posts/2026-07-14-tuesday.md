At the station the third counted the bags out of habit — one, two, three — and paused a beat too long afterward, as if the count should have gone one further. Nobody asked why. He carried nothing this time; there was nothing left to carry, and stood with his hands in his pockets like a man not used to that. She looked down the platform at the tracks and didn't look at either of them again until the train was already pulling in. They boarded in the order they always had, though for a moment, from a distance, it might have looked like there were more of them than three.
