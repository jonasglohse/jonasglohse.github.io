The chair had been dragged exactly four feet, into the one patch of sun the balcony still offered, and had not moved since. Lying in it counted, in some private accounting, as effort. The neighbor watering his tomatoes twice made eye contact and nodded, professional to professional, as if they'd both clocked in. Nothing else happened for an hour, which felt, on reflection, like the correct amount of things to happen. The sun did not seem to mind either.
