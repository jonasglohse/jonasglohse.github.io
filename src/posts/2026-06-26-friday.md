On Friday the heat returned. It came, as it always comes to this city, without asking permission and without any particular hurry. By the time it reached the room it had grown large enough to hold everything inside it — the furniture, the sounds, the distance between one hour and the next. I noticed at some point that I had stopped thinking about where I was. The heat had taken that from me, gently, the way you take something from a child who is already falling asleep.
