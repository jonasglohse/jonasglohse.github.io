On Saturday the square filled with people the way squares fill on days that have been designated for filling. A brass band played something that no one could quite identify but everyone recognized, and the recognition moved through the crowd like a slow current, producing in each person it touched a brief sensation of having been somewhere else entirely. I bought something wrapped in paper and ate it standing up. The sun was at an angle that made the flags — there were many flags, of no particular nation — look as though they were being seen for the first time. By late afternoon, the children who had arrived crying had stopped, and the ones who had arrived laughing had grown quiet, and everyone seemed equally satisfied with the exchange.
