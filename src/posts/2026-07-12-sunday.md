At the market stall, the third did the haggling this time, out of habit more than need, and paid the higher price anyway when the vendor didn't blink. He ended up carrying everything, the way he always did, without being asked and without offering the choice. She hung back at the edge of the stall, translating for no one in particular, and got a word wrong on purpose to see if either of them caught it. Neither did, or neither said so, and she bought the extra bag of dates by herself.
