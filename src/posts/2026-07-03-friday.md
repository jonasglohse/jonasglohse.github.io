I said a full goodbye to my neighbor at the elevator — the closing remarks, the well-wishes, a nod meant to end things — and then the doors did not open for eleven more seconds. We stood there, already finished, waiting for the building to catch up. She restarted the conversation from a lower register, as if the first goodbye had been a rehearsal. I agreed, at reduced volume, that yes, it was warm for July. The doors opened at the ground floor and we said goodbye again, properly this time, as though the first one had simply been misplaced. I smiled about it the rest of the way to my door.
