Something at the edge of the sky had started to gray, not enough to justify moving the chair, but enough to notice twice in ten minutes. The forecast app updated itself without being asked, which felt personal. Between one glance at the horizon and the next, an entire imaginary week of tasks assembled itself, filed itself, and then, mercifully, sat back down. The tomatoes next door looked unbothered. It was, by any reasonable measure, still a good afternoon — the kind you keep checking to make sure.
